FAQ Page

General Information & Contact

Q: How do I report a non-emergency issue?
A: The primary way is through the 311SA app, website, or by calling 311. The 311SA app allows you to report issues like potholes, submit photos, and track the status of your request in real-time. However, for life-threatening emergencies, always call 911 first.
Q: Who do I contact for after-hours emergencies?
A: For urgent issues like major water main breaks or downed trees blocking roads, call 311. The line is monitored for emergencies outside of standard business hours. For water or sewer emergencies, contact SAWS directly at (+1) 210-704-7297.

Streets, Sidewalks & Traffic

Q: How do I report a pothole?
A: Use the 311SA app, website, or call 311. Please provide the exact location (address or intersection) and, if safe, a photo. You can monitor the repair status through the app.
Q: How do I report a streetlight that is out?
A: Report it through 311SA. This service request will be routed to CPS Energy, which maintains street lights in San Antonio. Please note the pole number and nearest address if possible.

Trash, Recycling & Bulk Waste

Q: What is my trash and recycling schedule? How do I get a new cart?
A: You can find your personalized collection schedule and request a new or repaired trash or recycling cart through the City of San Antonio Solid Waste Department's website or by submitting a request via 311SA.
Q: Where is the nearest drop-off center?
A: The City offers scheduled bulk waste collections. Rules on acceptable items, pile sizes, and scheduling are available on the Solid Waste Department's website. For self-service, the Bitters Brush Recycling Center is the main drop-off facility for brush, bulky items, and some recyclables. Check the City website for hours, fees, and accepted materials.

Stormwater & Drainage

Q: What should I do if I see a clogged storm drain?
A: Please report clogged inlets or drainage issues through 311SA. Keeping storm drains clear of leaves and debris is crucial for flood prevention. Do not attempt to remove heavy debris yourself.
Q: Who is responsible for cleaning drainage easements?
A: The City maintains public drainage systems in the public right-of-way. Property owners are generally responsible for maintaining any drainage easements on their private property. If you're unsure, submitting a 311 request will get the issue to the correct department for assessment.

Construction & Permits

Q: How do I find out about upcoming road construction?
A: Major projects are listed on the City of San Antonio Public Works Department website. You can also sign up for traffic advisories and neighborhood notifications through the City's communications system.
Q: I'm a contractor. Where do I apply for a permit to work in the right-of-way?
A: Permits for work in the public right-of-way (ROW permits) are managed by the Public Works Department's Transportation Division. Applications and requirements are available on the City's official permitting portal.

Water & Sewer

Q: Who do I call for a water main break or sewer backup?
A: The San Antonio Water System (SAWS) manages the water and sewer system. Report emergencies 24/7 to SAWS Customer Service at (+1) 210-704-7297.
Q: What is the difference between Public Works and SAWS?
A: Public Works manages streets, drainage, traffic signs, and some public property. SAWS is the separate, public utility responsible for drinking water, wastewater treatment, and sewer lines.
